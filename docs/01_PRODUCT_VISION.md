---

## The Problem

Early-stage founders regularly sign vendor agreements, customer contracts, employment offers, partnership agreements, and investment documents without dedicated legal teams.

While AI contract review tools explain legal language and identify risky clauses, founders still struggle to understand the **business consequences** of those agreements.

A single poorly negotiated clause can expose an early-stage startup to financial liabilities, unfavorable renewal terms, or operational commitments that far outweigh the cost of the agreement itself.

This uncertainty leads to:

- Unfavorable negotiations entered without leverage
- Unnecessary financial exposure accepted unknowingly
- Strategic opportunities missed due to delayed decisions
- Agreements signed with incomplete confidence

This is not primarily a legal problem.  
It is a **business decision problem.**

---

## The Solution

Pactra is a Founder Decision Intelligence Platform.

It does not summarize contracts.  
It does not replace lawyers.  
It answers the question every founder actually has:

> *"Should I sign this — and if not, what do I do next?"*

Pactra achieves this by combining two complementary inputs into a unified decision workflow:

1. **Contract Intelligence** — deep analysis of the agreement
2. **Founder Business Context** — the startup's stage, risk appetite, revenue, and goals

Unlike traditional legal review platforms that treat every contract similarly, Pactra adapts its recommendations based on the founder's business context.

The output is not a report.  
It is a **decision** — with a recommended next action.

## Product Principles

These five principles are non-negotiable. They govern every feature, every AI output, and every design decision.

### Principle 1 — Business Decisions Over Legal Jargon
Pactra does not explain law. It explains business impact.  
Every AI output translates legal language into financial, strategic, and operational consequence.

### Principle 2 — Explain Before Recommending
The founder must always understand *why* before being told *what.*  
No recommendation is surfaced without reasoning. Confidence requires understanding.

### Principle 3 — Every Analysis Ends With an Action
Analysis never concludes with a summary.  
It concludes with a next step.  
*"Here's what you should do next"* — always.  
*"Analysis complete"* — never.

### Principle 4 — Context Changes Everything
The same clause carries different implications for a pre-revenue startup versus a Series A company.  
Founder context is not optional input. It is the engine that personalizes every recommendation.

### Principle 5 — AI Assists. Founders Decide.
Pactra provides decision support.  
It does not make decisions on behalf of the user.  
The founder remains in control. The AI increases their confidence to act.

## Scope Definition (V1)

### What Pactra Does
- Accepts founder business context as primary input
- Analyzes uploaded agreements (PDF, DOCX, TXT)
- Explains business impact of key clauses
- Produces a structured decision recommendation (Sign / Negotiate / Wait / Escalate)
- Generates negotiation guidance and clause suggestions
- Tracks decision history per founder

### What Pactra Does Not Do (V1)
- ❌ Provide legally binding legal advice
- ❌ Replace lawyers or legal counsel
- ❌ Automatically negotiate contracts on behalf of founders
- ❌ Electronically sign agreements
- ❌ Manage enterprise contract lifecycle (CLM)
- ❌ Generate contracts from scratch
- ❌ Handle litigation or courtroom workflows
- ❌ Integrate with enterprise ERP systems

## Why Now?

Three trends have converged to make Pactra both possible and necessary:

1. **Generative AI** has made high-quality contract understanding accessible outside of large legal teams.
2. **Startup formation** continues to accelerate globally, with more founders making consequential commercial decisions earlier and faster than ever before.
3. **Early-stage founders** increasingly operate without dedicated legal counsel, yet face agreements of growing complexity at every stage of growth.

Pactra exists at the intersection of these three trends.  
The technology is ready. The need is acute. The timing is now.
